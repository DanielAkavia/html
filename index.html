My trip in India

India is a country in south Asia and the second must populated country in the world.
It has a rich nature and is one of the most ethnically diverse countries in the world.
Religious minorities, including Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, and Jains.

In this site I will share with you my journey in India.

Manali

My first destination was Manali, a small tourist town in the mountaints of Himachal Pradesh. Manali is a popular tourist town in India. There I enjoyed great restaurants with authentic Indian food and a beautiful mountain view.

Parvati Valley

Parvati Valley is situated in the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. The valley is named after "Paravati", the beloved wife of the god Shiva. Along the valley, there are small villages, and some residents still live in traditional wooden buildings.

Kalga Villege

My second destination in India was Kalga, a small villege in Paravati valley.
This place was less crowded with tourists, with basic living conditions, including a few hours of electricity and magical view.
There I traveled in the area around the villege and ate the best Malai Kofta in India. Malai Kofta is a delicious dish of fried balls of potato and paneer (Indian cheese) in a creamy mild gravy. I spent 10 days in this peacful villege.

Pulga Fairy Forest

Pulga is a tiny villege in Paravati valley. It is extremely queit with fewer tourists.
There is no road to Pulga. No vehicle can reach there and it's only accessible on foot. Right next to the village there is a beautiful forest called the Fairy Forest.

Dharamsala

My next destination was Dharamshala, the winter capital city of Himachal Pradesh and the home of Dalai Lama and all the Tibetans in exile. It is a well-known centre for studying Buddhism and maditation. It is one of the major visited sites in Himachal Pradesh attracting scholars, pilgrims and tourists. In Dharamshala I learned and practiced meditation in Tushita, a Tibetan meditation center. I also visited Hindu temples and attended a jewelry and painting course.

Rishikesh

My last destination was Rishikesh, one of the holiest places for Hindus. There I enjoyed to travel along the Ganga river and attended the Puja ceremony. This is a religious ceremony in which they offer light, flowers, and water or food to the divine. This ritual performed by Hindus, Buddhists and Jains, and they also get strangers into their ceremony.
